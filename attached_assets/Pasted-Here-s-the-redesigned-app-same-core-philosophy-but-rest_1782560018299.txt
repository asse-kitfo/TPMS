The redesigned app — 4 screens, 1 engine
1. Hub (Home)

Big Start Session / End Session button.
If a trade is open: shows "In Trade" status with time elapsed and a manual "Check In Now" button (for whenever you want to self-report outside the scheduled ping).
Loss count and discipline streak, same as before — short and visible, no dashboard clutter.

2. Quick Plan (replaces the old Trade Gate)
Before entering a trade in MT5, optional 10-second entry — not a gate, not blocking, just a log:

Asset, Direction
Entry / Stop / Invalidation condition (one sentence: "what proves this idea wrong")
One tap: "Entering Now"

This starts "In Trade" mode. No sliders, no breathing requirement, no checklist — because you said the planning problem is already solved in the trader's head; this just timestamps it so check-ins later can reference it.
3. In Trade (the core screen — this is the app now)
While a position is open:

Quiet display: pair, direction, time elapsed, your invalidation condition. No P&L.
Check-in pings every 5 minutes, adaptive cadence:

🙂 Calm → next ping in 10 min, silent log
😐 Watching closely → gentle one-liner, next ping in 5 min
😬 Urge to act → 60-sec box breathing → "re-read your invalidation condition" → next ping in 2 min
😰 Anxious → 60-sec breathing → grounding question ("about this trade, or money in general?") → next ping in 2 min


Manual SOS button always visible: "I want to act" — runs the same 😬 branch on demand, for whenever the urge hits between scheduled pings.
Trade Closed button → logs outcome (Win/Loss/BE) and auto-attaches the full check-in timeline (🙂😐😬🙂) to that trade.

This screen is the product. Everything else supports it.
4. Journal & Insight (merged Journal + Analytics + Replay)

List of past trades: outcome, emotional timeline strip, your own note.
One key chart, not a dashboard of charts: win rate by worst emotional state hit during the trade. This is the single number that proves to you whether staying calm correlates with winning — your own evidence, not the app's claim.
Optional CBT reflection prompt at end of day, only if you want it — not forced.

What's left out completely now
No Execution Monitor (can't watch MT5), no Rules Checklist gate (you already know your rules), no readiness score, no verdict engine (STATE ALIGNED/HARD BLOCK language — there's nothing left to "verdict" since the app doesn't gate entry anymore). The backend shrinks from a scoring engine to basically: sessions, trades, check-ins, timestamps. Much smaller surface area, much easier to actually finish and use daily.
The single sentence version of the new app
A timer that asks "how are you, right now" every few minutes while you're in a trade, breathes you down when the answer is bad, and shows you afterward whether staying calm actually won you more money.
